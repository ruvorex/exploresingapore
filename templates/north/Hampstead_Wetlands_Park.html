Hampstead Wetlands Park

About Us

Developed by JTC and NParks, the 3.23 hectare Hampstead Wetlands Park is a green sanctuary and a place for rest and recreation for the surrounding community of Seletar Aerospace Park.

This park provides a conducive environment for plants, birds and other wildlife that inhabit freshwater ecosystems. Snags and the habitat islands are essential parts of the wetlandâ€™s ecology, providing birds and other wildlife with shelter, nesting spots and resting grounds away from human disturbances and predators. The habitat islands also provide additional surface area for wetland plants to colonise. Wetlands help to remove sediments from surface run-off when it rains, thus acting as a natural filter. .

As you stroll along the scenic boardwalk, enjoy nature up close at the viewing decks and look out for a variety of interesting flora and fauna.

Heritage Trail

Sightseeing at the Wetlands
The picturesque view of the wetlands are definitely amazing and a pond that are filled with fish, turtles, insects and water plants serve as shelter and habitat.

Explore the Broadwalk
The boardwalk serve as footpath for visitors who wish to around the pond and get close to nature. There are dedicated viewing decks to enjoy the interesting variety of plants and animal life. Visitors can leisurely walk around and discover some of the butterflies and dragonflies flying around the park.

Walk Around the Park
Going around the short trails in the park, visitors can see interesting facts on the flora and fauna. The park is filled with greenery with different types of plant species.

Visit the Heritage Tree
The Heritage Tree named Broad Leaf Mahogany Raintree is kind of tree that are commonly found in wooden furniture and can grow up to 40m high.
